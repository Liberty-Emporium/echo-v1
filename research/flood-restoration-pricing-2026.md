## Water Categories (IICRC Standard)

| Category | Source | Mitigation Rate | Notes |
|----------|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Cat 1 — Clean | Supply line break, clean overflow | $3.50/sf | Least expensive |
| Cat 2 — Gray | Appliance, dishwasher, washing machine | $5.25/sf | Moderate hazard |
| Cat 3 — Black | **Floodwater from outside, sewage, storm drain** | $7.50/sf + biohazard uplift | ALWAYS applies to flood claims |

> ⚠️ Any water that entered from outside (rain, storm surge, river) is **automatically Cat 3**. Never price flood claims at Cat 1 rates.

## Adjuster Red Flags

- **Current estimate under $8,000** with visible drywall peeling + flooring damage in photos → almost certainly underestimated
- **"Patch repair" on flood-damaged drywall** → insurance standard is full replacement when floodwater is involved
- **Missing mitigation phase** → extraction, drying equipment, and antimicrobial are separate from reconstruction and must be billed
- **Cat 1 rates on exterior flood** → any water from outside is Cat 3 by definition
- **No O&P line** → contractors require 20% overhead & profit; if it's not in the estimate, the homeowner pays it out of pocket
- **No contingency** → hidden damage (subfloor rot, wall cavity mold) is extremely common in flood claims
