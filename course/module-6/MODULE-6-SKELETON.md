## 📚 Lessons

### Lesson 6.1 — Presenting Your App (To Customers, Staff & Investors)
**Core Message:** The app is done. Now you have to sell people on using it.

**Script Topics:**
- Why adoption fails (people resist new tools — it's normal)
- The 2-minute demo: how to show your app to anyone in under 2 minutes
- Staff adoption: make it easier to use than NOT use it
- Customer adoption: frame it as a service upgrade, not a tech change
- The "before/after" story: how long did this take before? How long now?

**Slide Outline:**
1. Title: "Showing People What You Built"
2. The 2-minute demo framework
3. Staff adoption: the path of least resistance
4. Customer framing: "We built this for you"
5. Recording a Loom demo — tips and template
6. "The app isn't the hard part. The people are."

**Follow-Along Guide:**
**2-Minute Demo Script Template:**
```
"So here's what I built. [Open the app]

Before, when a customer wanted to [do X], they had to [old process].
It took [time], and we had to manually [painful step].

Now — [demo the app] — they just fill this out,
and we instantly get [result].

No phone tag. No missed messages. No lost orders.

This took [X hours/days] to build using AI. And we can
build one for [their business] too."
```

- [ ] Record a Loom demo of YOUR app using this script

- [ ] Send it to one potential user (staff member, business partner, customer)

### Lesson 6.2 — Your 90-Day AI Roadmap
**Core Message:** You didn't just build an app. You learned a process. The process scales.

**Script Topics:**
- The compounding effect: each app makes the next one faster
- Jay's roadmap: went from 0 to 10 apps in 6 months — how
- Month 1: Solidify what you built, get real users, gather feedback
- Month 2: Build your second app or upgrade your first
- Month 3: Connect your apps, start automating the automations
- The 10-app vision: what does your full business operating system look like?

**Slide Outline:**
1. Title: "Your 90-Day Roadmap"
2. Month 1: Ship → Use → Improve
3. Month 2: Expand — what's the second-highest automation opportunity?
4. Month 3: Connect — your apps start talking to each other
5. Jay's actual 10-app network (live screenshot)
6. "Where do you want to be in 90 days?"
7. Fill in your roadmap

### Lesson 6.3 — Monetizing What You Know
**Core Message:** You can use these skills to serve your own business. You can ALSO sell them to others.

**Script Topics:**
- Path 1: Internal — your business runs on AI, your competitors don't
- Path 2: Service — you build AI apps FOR other businesses (freelance/agency)
- Path 3: Products — you build and sell an app as a SaaS product
- Jay's path: built for himself → became the product → teaching others
- Pricing your service: what to charge, how to package it
- The "done-for-you" model: $500–$2,500 per app for small businesses
- Teaching others: this course as a product — the Skool opportunity

**Slide Outline:**
1. Title: "What You Know Is Worth Money"
2. The 3 paths — diagram
3. Path 1: Your business advantage (internal)
4. Path 2: Freelance/agency (serve others)
5. Path 3: SaaS product (sell an app)
6. Pricing guide for service work
7. "You walked in not knowing how to build an app. You're leaving with one running live."

**Follow-Along Guide:**
- Which path interests you most? Circle one: Internal / Service / Product / All Three
- If Service: write one sentence describing who you'd help and what you'd build for them
- If Product: write one sentence describing the app you'd sell and who'd buy it
- If Teaching: you now have the credibility — what would YOUR course be?
