# Platform Selection Reference

## Decision Framework

**Key questions:**
1. Do you want a standalone course platform or all-in-one (courses + email + community)?
2. How important is white-labeling / custom domain?
3. What's your expected monthly revenue? (Transaction fee vs. flat fee math matters)
4. Do you need community, assignments, or cohort features?

**Transaction fee math:** If a platform charges 5% on sales and you're doing $1,000/mo, that's $600/yr. A $49/mo flat-fee plan costs $588/yr. At $2,000/mo in sales the math flips hard against transaction fees.

## Platform Deep Dives

### Teachable
- Clean course player, solid student UX
- Integrated checkout with coupons, upsells, order bumps
- Built-in affiliate program
- Email marketing via Teachable (basic) or third-party integration
- Good for: creators who want marketing tools without leaving the platform

### Thinkific
- Best free tier in the market (1 course, unlimited students)
- Strong quiz, survey, and assignment features
- White-label: custom domain + branding on all plans
- Good for: beginners, educators who need robust assessment tools

### Kajabi
- Replaces 5+ tools: course platform + email marketing + sales funnels + landing pages + community
- Most expensive but total cost vs. stack comparison often favors it
- Built-in Kajabi Community (like Circle)
- Native video hosting, no Wistia/Vimeo needed
- Good for: creators serious about building a knowledge business

### Skool
- Simple, flat $99/mo regardless of students or revenue
- Community (forum) is the primary product; courses live inside the community
- Gamification (leaderboards, points) drives engagement
- No email marketing built in — you need an external ESP
- Good for: cohort-based programs, community-first courses, membership models

### Marketplace Platforms (Udemy, Skillshare)
**Pros:**
- Built-in audience, no marketing required to get first students
- Free to publish

**Cons:**
- You don't own your students' contact info
- Udemy discounts courses to $9.99–$19.99 routinely
- You're renting an audience, not building one
- Revenue share is poor (50%+ goes to platform on organic sales)

**Strategy:** Use Udemy/Skillshare to build credibility and testimonials, then migrate students to your own platform for premium offers.

## Features Checklist

When evaluating a platform, verify:

- [ ] Video hosting included (or do you need Wistia/Vimeo?)
- [ ] Drip content scheduling (release modules on a schedule)
- [ ] Assignment submission and feedback
- [ ] Quizzes and assessments
- [ ] Progress tracking (student and creator dashboards)
- [ ] Mobile-friendly student experience
- [ ] Custom domain support
- [ ] Payment plans and installments
- [ ] Coupon codes
- [ ] Affiliate/referral program
- [ ] Email integration (or built-in email)
- [ ] Community / discussion forums
- [ ] Live session support (Zoom integration or native)
- [ ] Analytics: enrollment, completion, revenue

## Recommended Starting Stack

**Bootstrap (under $100/mo total):**
- Thinkific (free or $48/mo) + ConvertKit free tier for email

**Growth ($100–200/mo):**
- Teachable Pro ($119/mo) or Kajabi Basic ($149/mo)

**Scale ($200+/mo):**
- Kajabi Growth ($199/mo) — all-in-one
- OR: Thinkific/Teachable + ActiveCampaign + Circle (community)
