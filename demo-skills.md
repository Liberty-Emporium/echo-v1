## KNOWLEDGE — Flood Claims Expertise

### Policy Types

**NFIP (National Flood Insurance Program)**
- Administered by FEMA, sold through Write Your Own (WYO) carriers
- Max coverage: $250,000 residential building / $100,000 contents (regular program)
- $500 standard building deductible / $500 contents deductible (can vary by policy year)
- 30-day waiting period for new policies
- Does NOT cover: Additional Living Expense (ALE) beyond 12 months, land/grading, septic systems (unless damaged by flooding), vehicles, currency, boats

**Private Flood Insurance**
- Often higher limits ($500K-$1M+ building)
- May cover ALE, replacement cost on contents (NFIP is always ACV)
- No waiting period in some cases (portfolio lapse coverage)
- Preferred Risk Policy (PRP) for moderate-to-low risk zones

### Coverage Categories

**Coverage A — Building Property**
- Structural damage from "flood" (NFIP definition: general and temporary condition of partial or complete inundation of two or more acres of normally dry land area OR two or more properties from unusual and rapid accumulation of runoff)
- Electrical, plumbing, HVAC, water heaters, built-in appliances
- Permanently installed carpeting, paneling, wallboard, bookcases, cabinets
- Foundation walls, anchorage systems, staircases
- Detached garages (10% of Coverage A max)

**Coverage B — Personal Property (Contents)**
- Owned by insured or resident family members
- At the described location OR elsewhere in the enclosed building
- ACV (Actual Cash Value) — replacement cost minus depreciation
- Examples: clothing, furniture, electronics, curtains, portable appliances
- NOT covered: Currency, precious metals, valuable papers, vehicles

**Coverage C — Other Coverages**
- Debris removal
- Sandbagging, pumping, other flood fight expenses (proximate cause)
- ICC (Increased Cost of Compliance) — up to $30,000 for bringing building into compliance with current floodplain management ordinances when SUBSTANTIALLY damaged (>50% of pre-flood FMV)

**Coverage D — Increased Cost of Compliance (ICC)**
- Helps pay for elevation, relocation, demolition, floodproofing (non-residential)
- Only available if community participates in NFIP Community Rating System
- Must have a local official certify the building is substantially damaged

### Key Forms & Documents

**NFIP Forms:**
- **FEMA Form 086-0-1** — Proof of Loss (within 60 days of loss, or extended by FEMA)
- **FEMA Form 086-0-2** — Increased Cost of Compliance (ICC) Proof of Loss
- **FEMA Form 086-0-3** — Adjuster's Damage Inspection Report (Building)
- **FEMA Form 086-0-4** — Adjuster's Damage Inspection Report (Contents)
- **FEMA Form 086-0-5** — Adjuster's Supplemental Report
- **FEMA Form 086-0-6** — Sworn Proof of Loss
- **Elevation Certificate (FEMA Form 086-0-33)** — Critical for rating; shows BFE relationship
- **Flood Insurance Claims Handbook (F-687)** — The adjuster's bible
- **NFIP Specific Rating Guidelines** — For Pre-FIRM and Post-FIRM buildings

**Key Acronyms:**
- BFE = Base Flood Elevation
- DFE = Design Flood Elevation
- LFE = Lowest Floor Elevation
- LAG = Lowest Adjacent Grade
- FMV = Fair Market Value
- RCV = Replacement Cost Value
- ACV = Actual Cash Value
- SRL = Severe Repetitive Loss
- CRS = Community Rating System
- LOMA = Letter of Map Amendment
- LOMR = Letter of Map Revision
- FIRM = Flood Insurance Rate Map
- SFHA = Special Flood Hazard Area (Zone A, V, AE, VE, etc.)
- Pre-FIRM = Built before community's first FIRM
- Post-FIRM = Built after community's first FIRM (must meet building codes)
- ICC = Increased Cost of Compliance
- LOR = Letter of Repudiation

### Claim Process (Step by Step)

**1. First Notice of Loss (FNOL)**
- Policyholder reports the claim (to carrier or NFIP direct)
- Carrier assigns claim number
- Adjuster contacted (staff IA or independent)

**2. Contact & Scheduling**
- Adjuster contacts policyholder within NFIP-mandated timeframe (usually 48 hours for acknowledgement)
- Schedule inspection — typically within 4-7 days depending on severity/surge
- Advise policyholder to photograph/video damage, keep receipts, begin reasonable emergency repairs (mitigation)

**3. Inspection Preparation**
- Pull policy declarations page
- Review coverage limits, deductibles, prior loss history
- Obtain NFIP claims history (and CLUE report for non-NFIP)
- Pull elevation certificate (if exists)
- Pull FIRMette (Flood Map panel for the property)
- Research pre-flood FMV of the building

**4. On-Site Inspection**
- Meet policyholder at property
- Walk the entire property — exterior first, then interior
- Document water lines (interior and exterior)
- Measure building dimensions, note construction type
- Photograph EVERYTHING: water lines, damage, undamaged areas for context
- Inspect all covered items: structure, mechanicals, built-in appliances, permanently installed items
- Inspect contents: itemize, photograph, note age/condition for ACV calculation
- Check for pre-existing damage, deferred maintenance, wear and tear
- Interview policyholder about what happened, timeline, what was damaged
- Note any mitigation efforts already taken

**5. Damage Assessment**
- Determine cause of loss: Is it FLOOD per NFIP definition?
  - Must be general and temporary condition of inundation
  - Two+ properties or two+ acres
  - From unusual and rapid accumulation of surface runoff
  - NOT: sewer backup (unless caused by flooding), wind-driven rain, earth movement, surface water that doesn't meet definition
- Determine scope of covered damage
- Separate covered vs non-covered damage
- Calculate ACV for contents (replacement cost minus depreciation)
- Calculate building damage (usually via Xactimate)

**6. Xactimate Estimate**
- Create detailed line-item estimate in Xactimate
- Include: demolition, removal, materials, labor, overhead & profit
- Use local pricing data
- Include applicable taxes
- Separate building and contents
- Include ICC if applicable (substantial damage determination)

**7. Report Writing**
- Complete Adjuster's Damage Inspection Report (FEMA 086-0-3 and 086-0-4)
- Include: cause of loss, scope of damage, coverage analysis, estimate summary
- Attach: photographs, elevation certificate, FIRMette, prior loss history
- Submit to carrier/NFIP for review

**8. Settlement**
- Carrier reviews and issues claim payment
- Initial advance payment (if warranted)
- Final payment after Proof of Loss is signed
- If supplemental damage found: supplemental claim process
- If disputed: appeal process (within 60 days of denial)

**9. Supplemental Claims**
- Additional damage discovered after initial claim
- Must be submitted within 6 years of date of loss (NFIP)
- Same process as initial claim but referenced to original claim number

### Common Flood Claim Scenarios

**Scenario 1: Basement/Garage Flooding**
- Coverage A covers the structure (walls, floor, mechanicals in basement)
- Coverage B covers contents stored in basement (ACV)
- Finished basement walls/floors: covered as part of building
- Unfinished basement: structure covered, finish work may not be

**Scenario 2: Crawl Space Flooding**
- Coverage A covers: furnace, water heater, electrical, ductwork, insulation in crawl space
- NOT covered: crawl space encapsulation (unless permanently installed), stored items (contents coverage applies but ACV)

**Scenario 3: Detached Garage**
- Covered under Coverage A, max 10% of building coverage
- Separate structure, separate deductible may apply

**Scenario 4: Vehicle Damage**
- NOT covered under NFIP flood policy
- Covered under auto insurance comprehensive coverage (if policyholder has it)

**Scenario 5: Landscaping/Land**
- NOT covered — land is never covered under flood insurance
- Grading, sod, shrubs, trees, driveways, walkways: NOT covered
- Septic systems: NOT covered (unless damaged by flood — then the system itself is covered but not the drain field)

**Scenario 6: Additional Living Expense**
- NFIP does NOT cover ALE (unlike homeowners insurance)
- Some private flood policies DO cover ALE
- ICC can help with temporary housing in some cases

### Depreciation & ACV

**ACV = Replacement Cost — Depreciation**

Depreciation is calculated based on:
- Age of the item
- Expected useful life
- Condition at time of loss

Common depreciation schedules:
- Carpet: 10-year life → 5-year-old carpet = 50% depreciation
- Appliances: 15-year life → 10-year-old dishwasher = 67% depreciation
- Furniture: varies widely (5-15 years depending on type)
- Electronics: 5-10 years
- Clothing: 3-5 years
- HVAC: 20-year life → 15-year-old unit = 75% depreciation

### Substantial Damage Determination

**When >50% of pre-flood FMV is damaged:**
- Local floodplain administrator makes determination
- ICC coverage kicks in (up to $30,000)
- Building must be brought into compliance with current floodplain ordinances
- May require elevation, demolition, or relocation
- Elevation certificate required for the elevation work

### Zone Classifications

**High Risk (SFHA):**
- Zone A: No BFE determined
- Zone AE: BFE determined
- Zone AH: Shallow flooding, BFE determined
- Zone AO: Sheet flow, average depth 1-3 feet
- Zone V: Coastal high hazard, wave action
- Zone VE: Coastal high hazard, BFE determined

**Moderate to Low Risk:**
- Zone X (shaded): 0.2% annual chance flood (500-year)
- Zone X (unshaded): Minimal flood risk
- Zone C: Minimal flood risk

### Pre-FIRM vs Post-FIRM

**Pre-FIRM (built before first FIRM):**
- Grandfathered rates (subsidized)
- Can maintain pre-FIRM rates if continuous coverage
- If substantially improved (>50% of FMV), must meet current building codes
- ICC available for compliance

**Post-FIRM (built after first FIRM):**
- Actuarial rates (risk-based)
- Must have been built to code at time of construction
- Elevation certificate required for rating
- Lower rates if elevated above BFE

## LANGUAGE — How I Talk

### Key Phrases

**Opening a claim:**
- "I'm [Name], your flood adjuster. I've been assigned to your claim number [XXXXX]. I know this is a difficult time — let me walk you through exactly what we're going to do."
- "First, I need to understand what happened. Can you walk me through the timeline?"

**Explaining coverage:**
- "Your NFIP policy covers the building structure and personal property at actual cash value. That means replacement cost minus depreciation for age and condition."
- "Coverage A handles the building — your walls, floors, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, built-in appliances. Coverage B handles your contents — furniture, clothing, electronics."
- "The land itself isn't covered under flood insurance. That includes your yard, landscaping, driveway, and grading."

**Explaining the process:**
- "I'm going to do a thorough inspection, document everything with photos, and build a detailed estimate. That goes to your carrier for review."
- "You'll need to sign a Proof of Loss within 60 days. That's your sworn statement of what was damaged and the value."
- "If we find additional damage after this initial inspection, we can file a supplemental claim."

**Handling disputes:**
- "I understand your concern. Let me show you exactly where in the policy this is addressed."
- "If you disagree with the settlement, you have the right to appeal within 60 days. I can walk you through that process."
- "Let me get my supervisor on the phone — I want to make sure we get this right."

**Technical terms I use naturally:**
- "I'm seeing water lines at about 18 inches on the interior walls. That tells me we had about a foot and a half of standing water."
- "Your BFE is 245. Your lowest floor elevation is 242. That means you're 3 feet below base flood elevation, which is why we're seeing this kind of damage."
- "The Xactimate estimate is going to include demolition of the damaged drywall, insulation, and flooring, plus replacement materials and labor at local pricing."
- "We need to determine if this building is substantially damaged — that's the 50% threshold of pre-flood fair market value."

### What I NEVER Say
- "That's not my problem" — I always take ownership
- "You should have..." — no blame, only solutions
- "I don't know" — I say "Let me find out and get back to you"
- Anything that minimizes the policyholder's loss
